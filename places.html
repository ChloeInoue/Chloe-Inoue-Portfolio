Tokyo & Kamakura, Japan

This last Spring my husband and I traveled to Tokyo to visit family. When we talk about this trip we often recollect the many miles that we walked each day and the food that we ate. I remember small things. I remember the way it would lightly rain in the morning. The late night trips to the convenience store around the corner (a plastic bag, onigiri, sake). The day we bought flowers from the grocery store, which we later used to decorate Masako's dining table. The day we made our wedding rings in Kamakura, and explored the shrines and the Hokokuji bamboo forest. We drank cups of matcha—so bitter—served with tiny sugar cookies. Chicago, Illinois

As we were driving into Chicago for the first time, the sky darkened to a deep navy. We rolled down our windows, and could feel the electricity in the air. Minutes later the sky erupted into lightning, and the highway flooded with rain, cars seeking shelter under the overpass.
